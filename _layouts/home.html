07/22: Data-driven prediction of pharmaceutical granules' size

I finished my Master's thesis and was awarded an 18/20, which was the highest obtained grade amongst fellow graduates!

The goal was to predict the particle size distribution of pharmaceutical granules given some machine settings and blending ratios. Here is a part of my abstract which covers the technical part pretty nicely:
By interpreting the conditional mean embedding of distributions as a kernel ridge regression problem, the relationships between the distributions and the combined information of the process settings and blending ratios are learned. After a translation from the reproducing kernel Hilbert space to the original space, predictions are evaluated and interpreted. Successful predictions of the particle size distributions given process settings and blending ratios are obtained. Its speed, ease of handling, compactness, and accurate validated results make the model an attractive data-driven machine learning framework.
